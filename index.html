About Me

MY BACKGROUND

Khadija Safdar is a graduate student in Zoology from the University of Education, Lahore. Her studies focus on various biological research areas such as cellular and molecular mechanisms of disease, host-pathogen interaction, medical parasitology, genetics and physiology to decode fundamental disease mechanisms that informs diagnostic strategies and provide the framework for research to overcome complex biological challenges like cancer progression and multidrug resistance.

Her research interests are in translational biomedical science with a particular focus on developing new treatments. She is motivated to turn basic research on host-pathogen interactions into practical clinical solutions. She aims to specialize in sustainable drug discovery and gain industry-related research and development experience to address global health issues.

She has also demonstrated exceptional leadership qualities and has participated in extracurricular activities that have helped both students and the community over time. Khadija enjoys teaching and mentoring.

Research Experience

Thesis: A Comparison of Antibacterial Potential of Silver Nano Particles Synthesized from Garlic and Clove Extract Against some Pathogenic Bacteria

(Manuscript is being prepared for publication)

Objectives:

  • Green synthesis of AgNPs using garlic [AgNPs (G)] and clove [AgNPs (C)] extracts as reducing agents.
  • Evaluation of their antimicrobial activities while testing on various pathogenic bacteria like Salmonella sp., E.coli, etc.
  • Used SEM analysis, UV-visible spectrum, and Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy to evaluate the morphology, formation, and chemistry of NPs, respectively.
  • Comparison of AgNPs prepared by both sources.

Results:

AgNPs(G) proved to have more antibacterial potential than AgNPs(C).
